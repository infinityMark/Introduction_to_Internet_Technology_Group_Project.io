BHMH2002 Introduction to Economics

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. explain the issues involved in the allocation of scarce resources for individual economic agents and the economy as a whole
  2. apply relevant economic knowledge to conduct economic analysis of the behaviour of firms and markets, and the effectiveness of government economic policy
  3. explain the issues relating to macroeconomics and their impacts on an economy
  4. apply relevant economic knowledge to enhance their understanding of other business subjects

Continuous Assessment: 50% + Examination: 50% = 100%

BHMH2003 Introduction to Marketing

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. analyse various marketing environments and identify opportunities, threats and other factors or issues affecting marketing planning and decisions.
  2. describe the principles of various areas in marketing such as customer value, consumer behaviour, marketing research, international marketing and ethical issues
  3. identify marketing problems and issues, and suggest marketing strategies which provide solutions to the marketing problems and is identified
  4. apply relevant marketing concepts, knowledge and theories to practical marketing situations.

Continuous Assessment: 50% + Examination: 50% = 100%

BHMH1021 Personal Financial Planning

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. generalise the importance of personal financial planning
  2. develop personal financial statements and plans
  3. acquire the skill to look at, evaluate, and make decisions abou financial alternatives and its relation to their financial needs
  4. describe the characteristics of various financial products, such a credit cards, consumer loans, insurance and investment products.

Continuous Assessment: 50% + Examination: 50% = 100%

BHMH2121 Intermediate Accounting

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. demonstrate a sound understanding of the conceptual framework of financial reporting, concepts and techniques of accounting
  2. evaluate current accounting practice in light of the accounting principles and standards
  3. apply generally accepted accounting principles to situations
  4. solve problems and issues in relation to corporate financial accounting

Continuous Assessment: 50% + Examination: 50% = 100%

SEHH2041 Applied Computing

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. outline information technology and its application in different disciplines
  2. describe the functions and features of computer hardware and software components
  3. explain the principles of computer networks and data communications
  4. describe the basic structure of a database system and its application
  5. discuss the basic functions of a computer operating system
  6. develop end-user computing and problem-solving skills for use in real-life situation

Continuous Assessment: 40% + Examination: 60% = 100%

SEHH2042 Computer Programming

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. develop computer programs in one or more high level language programming environment
  2. design and develop structured and documented computer programs
  3. explain the fundamentals of object-oriented programming and apply it in computer program development
  4. integrate the computer programming techniques to solve practical problems

Continuous Assessment: 50% + Examination: 50% = 100%

SEHH1069 Calculus and Linear Algebra

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. describe the concepts of limit and continuity of functions, derivatives of functions, indefinite and definite integrals
  2. evaluate limits, derivatives and integrals of functions in a single variable
  3. implement techniques of solving systems of linear equations
  4. perform basic operations of matrix algebra and determinants
  5. apply methods of calculus and linear algebra to solve a range of practical problems

Continuous Assessment: 40% + Examination: 60% = 100%

SEHH2240 Database System

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. describe the basic concepts of database systems
  2. describe the architecture and functioning of database management systems, as well as to be able to apply the associated tools and techniques
  3. apply the principles and practices of good database design and analysis
  4. demonstrate an understanding of the direction of database technology and their implication so as to manage and plan database system developments
  5. apply database technologies for lifelong learning, e.g., web databases

Continuous Assessment: 50% + Examination: 50% = 100%

SHDH2035 Values and Ethics in Daily Life

2 hours of lecture (online) + 1 hour of tutorial (f2f)

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. demonstrate a working knowledge of basic moral principles and theories, and the ability to identify moral disagreements in relation to common issues in applied ethics
  2. articulate the underlying values involved in the controversy of applied ethical issues
  3. identify the modern values integral to the organisation of modern society
  4. articulate the arguments in support of these values

Continuous Assessment: 100%

SHDH2024 Logic and Reasoning

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. recognise arguments presented in natural language
  2. apply logical techniques in assessing reasoning both in everyday life and in academic pursuits
  3. become more critically aware of fallacies in everyday reasoning

Continuous Assessment: 50% + Examination: 50% = 100%

SHDH2043 Digital Storytelling

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. demonstrate an innovative way to solve communicative problems in a variety of disciplines
  2. describe the conceptual framework of digital storytelling on interdisciplinary relationships
  3. identify the applications of digital storytelling
  4. apply essential techniques in digital storytelling in terms of narrating, editing and sharing
  5. handle digital storytelling as an effective communication channel

Continuous Assessment: 100%

SHDH2048 The History and Culture of Modern China

2 hours of lecture + 1 hour of tutorial

Upon completion of this subject, students will be able to

  1. describe major socio-political-cultural changes in modern Chinese history
  2. explain some key conceptual tools for understanding modern China, such as reform, revolution, imperialism, nationalism, etc
  3. evaluate the comparative strengths and weaknesses of Chinese and Western cultures and their interactions
  4. apply relevant knowledge and concepts to explain contemporary Chinese issues such as the strong force of Chinese nationalism.

Continuous Assessment: 100%
